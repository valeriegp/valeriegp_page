bio

Valerie Guinn Polgar is a military brat born in El Paso, Texas who has a very difficult time answering the question "where are you from". She attended 5 elementary schools, 1 middle school, and 2 high schools. Valerie spent every summer (and some winters) visiting family in Chihuahua, Mexico, until she went to college in the early 2000s.

Valerie has a BFA in Digital Art from George Mason University, an MFA in Computer Art from the School of Visual Arts, and is currently pursuing an MS in Computer Science at Tufts University. She did video editing and motion graphics in NYC in the early 2010s before turning her attention to practicing and teaching art. She served as a professor/instructor in several universities/colleges in both art and computer science departments in New Jersey, Virginia, and Mississippi until 2020.

Valerie has been the head programmer of experimental film and animation for the Oxford Film Festival (OxFilm) since 2017. She also founded and ran a media arts entity called Misbits NMA from 2016 - 2020, which promoted art and technology through collaboration in Oxford, MS. Her current interests involve analyzing the role of contemporary digital/new media artists against the role that emerging tools can play in both shaping and eliminating these identities. She currently resides in Brookline, MA with her partner and two kids.
